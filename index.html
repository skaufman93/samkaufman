SAM KAUFMAN

COSO HOT SPRINGS

Sacred Lands, health resort, naval test station, the contingent identities surrounding the Coso Volcanic Range are in a constant state of entropy and fossilization. Tremulous formations and management agencies facilitate the stage for spiritual cleansing, material extraction, and place-making. This new architecture utilizes myself for performative operations found in designing/casting Spolia and Kintsugi. An aesthetic reference for recycling and re-identifying ruins, geologic forms, and ceramic parts.


Although the site is off limits to the public, documentary scenes of the hot springs (archival film, 1930, Somewhere on the Edge of Nowhere, 2005, Talking Stone, Rock Art of the Cosos, 2014) were collected and interpolated as forensic evidence. By extracting and compositing archival postcards, documentary sequences, USGS Lidar scans, the resulting scenography becomes a means of superimposing history with materiality.
